Parenting

Parenting disputes & custody

Fathers in Australia can secure substantial, enforceable time with their children. The Family Law Act presumes a child has the right to a meaningful relationship with both parents — our job is making that real for you.

We help you reach parenting arrangements through negotiation or Family Dispute Resolution (mediation) first, because agreements parents make tend to last longer than orders judges impose. Where agreement isn't possible, we apply for parenting orders and represent you through every stage of the Federal Circuit and Family Court — interim hearings, family reports, Independent Children's Lawyers, and final hearings if it comes to that.

Separation

Separation & divorce

The decisions you make in the first weeks of separation — about the house, the kids' routine, money — shape everything that follows. Get advice before you agree to anything, not after.

We advise on your rights and obligations from day one: living arrangements, interim parenting schedules, financial disclosure, and the divorce application itself (available 12 months after separation). Early, clear advice routinely prevents the expensive disputes that come from informal arrangements going wrong.

Urgent matters

Recovery & relocation orders

If your child has been taken or withheld, or your ex plans to move them away, the court can order their return or prevent the move — and these applications can be made urgently. Time matters: contact us the day it happens.

A recovery order directs that a child be returned to you and can authorise police to find and return them. Relocation disputes determine whether one parent may move a child far enough to disrupt your time together. Both turn heavily on acting fast and presenting the right evidence — exactly the kind of matter we handle week in, week out.

Support

Child support

Child support is set by a formula — but the formula's inputs can be wrong, and assessments can be challenged. If your assessment doesn't reflect your real income, your care percentage, or your circumstances, you can do something about it.

We assist with Services Australia assessments, objections and reviews, change-of-assessment applications, and private binding or limited child support agreements that give both parents certainty. We make sure what you pay — or receive — is actually fair.

Finances

Property settlement

Property is not automatically split 50/50 in Australia. The division depends on contributions, future needs, and what's just and equitable — which means how your case is argued genuinely changes the outcome.

We handle the full settlement process: identifying and valuing the asset pool (including superannuation and businesses), negotiating division, and formalising the outcome so it's final and enforceable. Strict time limits apply — 12 months after divorce, 2 years after a de facto separation — so don't let a settlement drift.

Agreements

Consent orders & binding financial agreements

An informal agreement with your ex is not enforceable — and can be walked back at any time. Consent orders and binding financial agreements turn your agreement into something the law will stand behind.

Consent orders are approved by the court without anyone attending and carry the same force as orders made after a trial. Binding financial agreements can be made before, during, or after a relationship. We draft and review both, and we'll tell you plainly which suits your situation — and when an agreement on the table is one you shouldn't sign.
